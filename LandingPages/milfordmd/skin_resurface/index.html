Birthmarks

Birthmarks are abnormalities on the skin that are either due to vascular irregularities, such as when blood vessels do not form correctly, or pigmentation irregularities, usually when there are more than one pigment (or color) in your skin’s DNA. Treatment will vary depending on which type of birthmark you are seeking to remove or reduce. In most cases, a laser treatment will improve the appearance of birthmarks.

Moles & Early Diagnosis

Moles and their early diagnosis can be found in the National Cancer Institute (NCI) booklet (NIH Publication No. 99-3133), which will help you learn more about common moles and unusual ones called dysplastic nevi or atypical moles. This booklet shows what moles look like and explains how they may be related to melanoma, a type of skin cancer. It describes the signs of melanoma and explains how you can check your skin for moles that might be cancerous. It also explains why and how you can protect your skin.

Port Wine Stains

Port wine stains are caused by a vascular anomaly, which causes a capillary malformation on the skin. They are considered birthmarks and their growth is in relation with the child’s growth. Port wine stains are generally found on the scalp, face, neck, arms, and legs – but can grow anywhere on the body. If left untreated, port-wine stains tend to darken over time as the blood flow through them becomes more sluggish. At first the overlying skin is smooth and flat, but over time the overlying skin can thicken and become lumpy.

Aesthetic laser treatments are generally recommended to improve the appearance of port wine stains. Treatment must be repeated for optimal results. Depending on the severity of the birthmark, surgery may be required.

Sun Damage & Sun Spots

Sun damage, including sun spots, is caused by the over-exposure of harmful ultraviolet (UV) sun rays. Sunbathing and constant sun exposure can put you at risk for abnormal skin discoloration, wrinkling and skin cancer. When your skin darkens, to what we believe is a tan, it is actually a sign of damage to the skin.

See the Sun Damage You Can’t See in the Mirror

UV photography provides physicians with an effective way to show patients the full extent of the sun’s impact on their skin. The selective absorption of UV light by epidermal melanin makes it easy to capture an image of the mottled hyperpigmentation that is characteristic of photodamage. The results can often be quite shocking for patients, heightening their awareness of the need for sun protection and raising a desire for treatment. A post-treatment UV photo compared to pre-treatment shows the patient what’s been accomplished.

Warts & Cysts

Warts and cysts are growths on the skin that are visually unpleasing. Warts may develop on any part of the body including the face, scalp, lips, nose and genital area as well as on the hands, feet, elbows and knees. Cysts can occur anywhere on the body but commonly appear on the face and scalp, trunk and fingernails. Before considering the removal of these skin lesions, the cosmetic dermatologists at MilfordMD must first determine whether the skin growth is benign or malignant in nature.
