Sexual wellness can be defined as a blend of physical, mental, and social well-being that connects with sexuality. Growing awareness regarding sexual wellness inspires a positive and respectful approach involving sexuality and sexual relationships. Sexual wellness includes the regulation of hormones and keeping the body in balance. Every system in our body plays an important role in our overall wellness and when one system, like our hormones or sexual wellness is off balance, our entire body can be thrown off track.

Sexual wellness treatments for men

Gainswave: This treatment option uses high-frequency acoustic shockwave therapy to increase blood flow to the penis, remove micro plaque and stimulate the growth of new blood vessels. Breaking up the plaque formation in the blood vessels and stimulating the growth of new blood vessels in the penis increases the overall blood flow to the penis.

P Shot: It’s a simple, nonsurgical procedure where platelet-rich plasma (PRP) from your blood is injected into your penile tissues. This is done to promote penile tissue growth resulting in better erections.

PDE5 inhibitors: Better known as Viagra, Cialis, Levitra, and Stendra, these are all a class of prescription medications that relax the penis muscle and encourage blood vessels to widen, making blood flow more freely, including to the penis.

Sexual wellness treatments for women

Femiwave: A procedure comparable to Gainswave for men, acoustic wave therapy is used to increase blood flow, and restore sensation and natural lubrication to the vagina.

O Shot: It’s a simple, nonsurgical procedure where platelet-rich plasma (PRP) from your blood is injected into specific areas around the vagina. The platelets naturally attract your own stem cells to the injected area and generate healthier and more functional tissue in the areas of sexual response in the vagina.

Vyleesi: A prescription drug approved by the FDA to treat a lack of sexual desire in pre-menopausal women. It’s injected in the abdomen or thigh at least 45 minutes before you intend to have sex. Results have been shown to improve a woman’s self-reported feelings of desire and lowering distress around having sex.
