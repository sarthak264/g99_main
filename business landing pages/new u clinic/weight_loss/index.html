What is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a synthetic GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide receptor agonist). It is an FDA-approved once-a-week injectable medication used to lose weight among patients with a body mass index greater than 30 or greater than 27 with comorbidities. People with type 2 diabetes and obesity have lower levels of this peptide than normal. A GLP-1 receptor agonist works by stimulating the pancreas to produce ideal amounts of insulin to help move sugar from the blood to the tissues, where it can be used for energy.
