Radio Frequency Microneedling

Radiofrequency microneedling (RF microneedling) is a skin rejuvenation treatment to trigger collagen and provides radiofrequency energy to improve skin texture tone. The combination of RF and microneedling also improves skin tone and texture, addresses fine lines and wrinkles, decreases acne scars, and improves laxity.

The treatment is designed to create controlled microdamage or tiny wounds in your skin that provoke a wound-healing response.

We find that aging and environmental damage – particularly sun damage – causes the skin’s structural proteins to be depleted and be replaced at a slower rate. RF microneedling gives the biological process of cell renewal a way to create new collagen and elastin.

Microneedling is a perfect treatment to rejuvenate the skin.

Microneedling provides the delivery of radiofrequency energy into your skin, where collagen-producing cell life begins. The radiofrequency energy increases the level of controlled wounds that microneedles give.

Microneedling creates a degree of damage that triggers rejuvenation but adding radio frequency to microneedling adds much more. The immune response is stimulated to a higher degree, and in return, a higher collagen repair response is triggered.

Microneedling with radiofrequency is a safe treatment and only has temporary side effects. If you do see pinprick marks on your skin, these will fade quickly within two weeks. When done correctly, RF and microneedling cause only controlled micro damage to your skin.

RF with microneedling stimulates repair responses in your skin to replace damaged elastic tissues and collagen. After a series of about three or four treatments, you will see a firmer, brighter, and smoother complexion.

The entire process RF microneeding provides a dramatic change that has better results than traditional microneedling or radiofrequency treatments alone. The combination of these two technologies boosts their effectiveness.

Before & After

Results of RF with microneedling can last for one or even two years. It all depends on you and your aesthetician. You could see improvements for longer depending on what area is being treated. Suppose you are treating acne scars; improvements may last many years. Photodamage and skin aging may not last as long, but you could see improvement for up to a year if you use the proper skincare plans.
