Microneedling

Microneedling or collagen induction therapy involves repeatedly puncturing the skin with extremely fine sterile needles. It sounds invasive, but it really is a minimally invasive procedure designed to smooth away fine lines, wrinkles, create a more even skin tone, and reduce the appearance of scars and blemishes.

Microneedling stimulates your body to produce new collagen and encourages cell turnover. It is a successful treatment for skin conditions like melasma, skin pigmentation issues, rosacea, acne issues, hair loss, large pores, sun damage, and loose skin.

Microneedling is a perfect treatment to rejuvenate the skin.

Microneedling is used to increase collagen and other healing factors by causing disturbance to the skin.

Collagen is an essential protein that keeps the skin looking youthful, firm, and smooth. Collagen is also lost because of injuries, stretch marks, acne scarring, or other scarring.

Aging decreases collagen production in the skin, which causes signs of aging like wrinkles.

Microneedling is not a quick fix. It involves the growth of new skin, which can take several months before the full results of the procedure are seen.

The entire process takes about 30 minutes, and once your provider uses a topical anesthetic, there is little pain. You may need four to six treatments to see a difference in your skin. Talk to your aesthetician about how many treatments you need.

Microneedling is usually done on the face, but it can be done on other areas of our body, like the stomach or thighs. Microneedling is safe and effective and can be less expensive than other rejuvenation treatments.
