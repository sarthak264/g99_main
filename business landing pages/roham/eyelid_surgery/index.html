Eyelid Surgery

A blepharoplasty, more commonly known as eyelid surgery, is a procedure aimed to improve and rejuvenate the eyes. Depending on the area of concern, you can have lower eyelid surgery, upper eyelid surgery, or both! Lower eyelid surgery focuses on eliminating the baggy fat pockets from underneath the eye. Upper eyelid surgery removes excess skin and fat, improving the appearance of the upper eyelids

Before & After

Dr. Roham makes tiny incisions in the natural creases of your eyelids to leave virtually no visible scarring. Whether you choose to undergo upper or lower eyelid surgery, both will help in restoring a more youthful and refreshed appearance.

During the upper eyelid procedure, excess skin, muscle, and fatty tissue are removed. Many people are bothered by their hooded eyelids. If you are bothered by the appearance of your hooded eyelids or it is starting to impair your vision, consider upper eyelid surgery.

Upper and lower eyelid surgery can be performed at the same time. Dr. Roham utilizes different types of surgical techniques to avoid the operated look. He is known for avoiding the “operated look” and delivers refreshed natural-looking results. His exceptional eyelid surgery results bring patients from all over California to our office.

Dr. Roham is an expert in treating puffy eyes and removing the bags under the eyes. This is also a very popular procedure in our office. The incisions are made in the natural creases under the eyes and are practically invisible. The excess fat and skin can be removed through the incision and a tiny absorbable stitch is placed to close the incision. Recovery is generally between 5-6 days.
