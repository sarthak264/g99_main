Breast Augmentation

Breast augmentation, sometimes referred to as breast enhancement or breast enlargement, is a surgical procedure in which breast implants are used to improve the size and shape of a woman’s breasts. It is one of the most common plastic surgery procedures, and the satisfaction rate is very high.

Women of all ages choose to have breast augmentation for many reasons, including underdeveloped, uneven, or sagging breasts. You can make many changes to your body by making healthy choices, but if you are dissatisfied with the size of your breasts, surgery may be your only option. Many women want to increase the volume of their breasts to look and feel better, and most are ecstatic about their improved appearance and boosted self-esteem after augmentation.

If you’d like to improve the size of your breasts or a curvier figure, then you’re an excellent candidate for breast augmentation. This treatment can also balance the asymmetry between breasts or restore volume after pregnancy and nursing. Some women may also wish to have a breast lift at the same time to further improve the appearance of the breasts. Your anatomy will help dictate the best strategy for achieving the breast size and shape you desire. During your consultation, Dr. Roham will use his expertise to guide your decision.

Implants come in different profiles that are based on the appearance of the implant from the side. High profile breast implants produce a rounder appearance and more outward projection; they work better for women who have loose or droopy skin or who desire more projection. Moderate profile breast implants create a more natural shape and appearance and are commonly used for women with tighter skin and smaller breasts and wish for a more subtle natural look.

Dr. Roham uses a specialized technique with highly advanced instruments to place your implants precisely and give you the natural look you want. His gentle technique allows for minimal bruising, decreased discomfort, a fast recovery, and a beautiful result. The surgery is performed under general anesthesia or IV sedation
