Veneers

Perry Family Dentistry offers handcrafted, custom veneers to help you enhance your smile. Our process is unique; from the test drive to the design, let us show you how you can smile again! Veneers transform the appearance of teeth by changing their color, shape, and size of teeth. The purpose of veneers is to cover the natural teeth and solve problems such as stains, chips, decay, and irregularities. You can select customized porcelain or composite veneers with the most advanced technology.

What Are Veneers?

Veneers are an effective way to enhance the appearance of your teeth. Your smile and laughter will be filled with confidence as a result.

Composite resin or porcelain are two common materials used in dental veneers. Both are long-lasting materials and will be made to match the color and feel of your toothâ€™s enamel. As part of your consultation with your dentist at Perry Family Dentistry, you can discuss the shade of veneers that are most suitable for you.

What Is The Purpose Of Veneers?

Dental veneers are used to fix teeth for a variety of reasons. Veneers can fix crooked, chipped, discolored, or stained teeth. Veneers may also be required by people with misaligned teeth or teeth with large gaps between them.

How Are Veneers Placed?

Veneers are applied once your teeth have been prepared by the dentist. When necessary, the dentist will remove some enamel and place dental fillings to create space for veneers. To create the perfect restoration for you, a dental impression will be taken once they are ready. Until your custom veneers are ready, you can wear temporary veneers.

The custom veneers will be placed precisely by your dentist at your next appointment so that you can have a beautiful, bright smile afterward. Any minor adjustments can be made at this point.

How Long Do Veneers Typically Last?

Dental veneers have a lifespan that varies depending on whether they are porcelain or composite. It is possible to expect porcelain laminate veneers to last between 10 and 12 years, while composite resin veneers typically last for four to eight years, so they will need to be replaced sooner.
